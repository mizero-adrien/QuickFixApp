## Personal Reflection

Before this assessment, I understood what a marketplace app was in theory. What I did not understand was how much work goes into justifying why it should exist. This milestone taught me that a good app concept is not born from a clever idea — it is born from listening carefully to real people and letting their frustrations guide every decision.

The moment that stayed with me most from this project happened during my field research in Kimironko. I interviewed an electrician who had been sitting outside a hardware shop every morning for years, waiting for clients who might or might not show up. He told me: "Some days I go home with nothing." That sentence became the foundation of our entire artisan-side value proposition steady bookings, a digital profile, reliable MoMo payment. Everything we designed for artisans traces back to that one conversation.

My teammate and I worked well together throughout. Our roles were clearly defined from the start I owned the wireframes, the written documentation, and the UI design direction. My teammate owned the technical architecture. This clarity meant we rarely blocked each other. When timelines slipped slightly, we communicated openly and adjusted without conflict.

The wireframing process pushed me more than I expected. My first designs were too complex for a nine-week Flutter build, and I had to simplify significantly. That constraint made the final designs cleaner and more focused — something I would not have discovered without the pressure of a realistic timeline.

I am proud of the concept we built and confident it solves a real problem.
