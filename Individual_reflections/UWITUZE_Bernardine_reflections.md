## Personal Reflection

This assessment was one of the most valuable learning experiences I have had in this course so far. Going into it, I assumed the hardest part would be the technical side — figuring out the app architecture and the Flutter implementation plan. What I did not expect was that the field research would turn out to be the most important part of everything we did.

When I went to Gasabo and interviewed a landlord who had lost 15,000 RWF to an artisan who disappeared after taking cash upfront, the problem became real to me in a way that no assignment brief could communicate. That single conversation shaped every technical decision I made afterward  especially the choice to make MoMo escrow payment the core feature rather than just a nice addition.

Working with my teammate went smoothly from the beginning. We divided responsibilities clearly I handled the technical architecture, Flutter implementation planning, and GitHub setup while my teammate focused on the UI design and written documentation. There were moments where we disagreed on scope, particularly on whether to include live map tracking in the MVP. We resolved this by going back to our research — our interviewee had specifically requested it and that discipline of letting the evidence decide rather than personal preference was something I will carry into future projects.

The biggest challenge was scoping realistically. I had to keep reminding myself that we only have nine weeks and Flutter is still relatively new to both of us. Constraints forced better decisions.

I feel genuinely ready to build this app.
