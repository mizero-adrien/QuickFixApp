# Field Research — QuickFix App
**Date:** March 27, 2026
**Location:** Kigali, Rwanda
**Team Members:** Member 1 | Member 2

## Research Overview

**Research Goal:** Understand how people in Kigali currently find and hire home service artisans (plumbers, electricians, painters, carpenters), and identify the key friction points in that process.

**Method:** Independent observation (10–15 minutes) + 1 user interview per team member.


## Member 1 [Bernardine UWITUZE] — Field Research

**Location:** Residential area, Gasabo District, Kigali
**Date & Time:** March 25, 2026 | 10:00 AM – 10:20 AM
**Observation Duration:** 15 minutes

### Observation Notes

| Time | Observation |
|------|------------|
| 10:00 AM | Arrived at a residential compound. Noticed a landlord speaking with a worker about a broken water pipe. |
| 10:05 AM | Landlord was calling multiple contacts on his phone trying to find a plumber. Made 4 calls before getting a response. |
| 10:10 AM | Worker who arrived was not a plumber — he was a general handyman referred by a neighbour. No formal qualification visible. |
| 10:15 AM | Observed cash exchanged upfront before work began. No receipt or written agreement. |
| 10:20 AM Spoke briefly with the landlord who expressed frustration about not knowing if the worker was qualified. |

### User Interview

**Interviewee:** Landlord, male, 45 years old, manages 3 rental properties in Gasabo
**Interview Duration:** 12 minutes

**Key Quotes:**

> "When something breaks, I just call whoever I called last time. If they don't pick up, I ask my neighbour."

> "I have no way of knowing if the person is actually qualified. I just hope they know what they are doing."

> "I paid 15,000 RWF upfront and the guy didn't come back to finish the job. I lost that money."

> "I would love an app where I can see who is available near me and read reviews before I call anyone."

> "Sometimes I wait two or three days just to find someone. That is very stressful when there is water leaking."

### Friction Points Identified

| # | Friction Point | Evidence |

* No reliable way to find qualified artisans | Landlord made 4 calls before getting any response |
* No way to verify artisan qualifications or skills | Handyman arrived instead of plumber with no way to know beforehand |
* Cash paid upfront with no guarantee of completion | Lost 15,000 RWF when artisan did not return |
* Long waiting times to find available help | Sometimes waits 2–3 days during an active emergency |
* No reviews or ratings system to assess quality | Must rely entirely on word of mouth from neighbours |
